PARALLAX

Parallax is an effect where the background content or image in this case, is moved at a different speed than the foreground content while scrolling. Check out the demo to get a better idea of it.

MODALS

Use a modal for dialog boxes, confirmation messages, or other content that can be called up. In order for the modal to work you have to add the Modal ID to the link of the trigger. To add a close button, just add the class .modal-close to your button.

Trivia for Coffee

Coffee beans are actually seeds: The coffee "bean" is the pit or seed of a bright red or purple fruit called a coffee cherry. Coffee is one of the most traded commodities: It is second only to oil in terms of its global economic importance. The word "coffee" has various origins: The word "coffee" is believed to have originated from the Arabic word "qahwah" or the Turkish word "kahve." Coffee was discovered in Ethiopia: According to legend, coffee was discovered by a goat herder named Kaldi in Ethiopia in the 9th century. He noticed that his goats became energetic after eating the berries from a certain tree. Brazil is the world's largest producer: Brazil is the largest producer of coffee, responsible for about one-third of the world's total coffee supply. Espresso is not a type of coffee bean: Espresso is a brewing method, not a type of coffee bean. It is made by forcing hot water through finely ground coffee under high pressure.

Trivia for Milktea

Origin of milk tea: Milk tea, also known as bubble tea or boba tea, originated in Taiwan in the 1980s. It was created as a variation of traditional Taiwanese tea by adding milk and tapioca pearls. Tapioca pearls: The signature element of milk tea is the tapioca pearls, also known as boba. These small, chewy balls are made from tapioca starch and are typically added to the tea for a unique texture and flavor. Varieties of milk tea: Milk tea comes in a variety of flavors and types. Some popular options include classic milk tea, matcha milk tea, taro milk tea, and fruit-flavored milk tea. Bubble tea's popularity: Milk tea has gained immense popularity worldwide, especially among younger generations. It has become a trendy and widely consumed beverage in many countries.

Trivia for Corndog

Invention of the corndog: The corndog, also known as a corn dog or a Pronto Pup, is believed to have been invented in the United States. Its exact origin is disputed, but it is often credited to two men: George F. N. McFarland and Carl and Neil Fletcher. They independently introduced the corndog at the Texas State Fair and the Iowa State Fair, respectively, in the 1940s. The corndog's batter: The traditional corndog batter is made by combining cornmeal, flour, sugar, salt, and buttermilk. The hot dog is skewered with a stick, dipped in the batter, and deep-fried until golden brown. Popular fair food: Corndogs are commonly associated with fairs, carnivals, and amusement parks. They have become a classic staple of fairground cuisine and are enjoyed by people of all ages. International variations: While corndogs are most commonly associated with the United States, variations of this deep-fried treat can be found in different parts of the world. For example, in South Korea, there is a popular street food called "hotteok," which is a sweet pancake filled with ingredients like sugar, cinnamon, and nuts, and can also include a hot dog.
